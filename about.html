I am a conservation scientist and PhD candidate at the University of Cambridge, researching how to reconcile biodiversity conservation with the needs of human communities. My work focuses on humanâ€“wildlife conflict and landscape-scale conservation, applying spatial and statistical modelling to integrate field observations, satellite imagery, and species movement data. Through this, I seek to predict where, when, and how people and wildlife are most likely to come into conflict, with a particular emphasis on African landscapes. I hold an MSc in Conservation from University College London and a BA in Geography from the University of Exeter. I previously worked as a research assistant at the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew, and carried out fieldwork in South Africa and in the Congo Basin. I have also contributed to conservation and research projects in Sierra Leone, Ethiopia, Madagascar, and the UK.
